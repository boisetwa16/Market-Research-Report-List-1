<p><p>Molecular spectroscopy encompasses various techniques for analyzing molecular properties based on their interaction with electromagnetic radiation. NMR (Nuclear Magnetic Resonance) spectroscopy provides insights into molecular structure through magnetic properties of nuclei. UV-Visible spectroscopy measures absorbance in the ultraviolet and visible ranges, helping identify compounds and assess concentration. Infrared spectroscopy detects molecular vibrations, revealing functional groups and chemical bonds. Near-Infrared spectroscopy, similar to infrared, focuses on overtones and combinations of fundamental vibrations, useful in food and pharmaceutical analysis. Together, these techniques enhance molecular characterization across industries.</p></p>
