<p><p>Mass spectrometry in the clinical market involves two primary types: single mass spectrometry and mixed mass spectrometry. Single mass spectrometry focuses on analyzing individual compounds, providing precise qualitative and quantitative data for specific analytes. In contrast, mixed mass spectrometry combines multiple mass spectrometric techniques or platforms, enabling comprehensive analysis and increased sensitivity to detect complex biomolecules. Both types play vital roles in diagnostics, drug development, and toxicology, enhancing clinical decision-making and patient care through detailed molecular insights.</p></p>
