<p><p>The automotive parking brake market includes two main types: Drum Brake Type and Disc Brake Type. Drum brake parking brakes use a drum and shoe mechanism, commonly integrated into rear wheels, offering cost-effectiveness and simplicity. Disc brake parking brakes, often called caliper or electronic systems, use a disc and caliper setup, providing better performance, heat dissipation, and compatibility with modern vehicles. Both types ensure vehicle stability when parked, with drum brakes being traditional and disc brakes increasingly favored for advanced features and efficiency.</p></p>
