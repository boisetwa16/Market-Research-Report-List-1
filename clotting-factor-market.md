<p><p>The clotting factor market is segmented into various types based on specific clotting factors. Clotting Factor I, also known as fibrinogen, plays a crucial role in blood coagulation. Clotting Factor II, or prothrombin, is essential for the conversion of fibrinogen to fibrin. Clotting Factor III, known as tissue factor, initiates the clotting process. Clotting Factor IV refers to calcium ions, vital for several steps in the coagulation cascade. The "Others" category encompasses less common factors and related therapeutic products.</p></p>
